# Stage 1 — Problem

**Tool:** ChatGPT or Claude (not Lovable yet)
**Goal:** Understand the problem you're solving before you describe any
screens, features, or apps.

## Why this stage exists

It's tempting to jump straight into an AI app builder and start "vibe
coding" — describing a screen and seeing what comes out. That works, but it
tends to produce an app that *looks* like a product without solving anything
specific for anyone specific. This stage forces a pause: who is this for,
what are they doing today instead, and why would they switch.

You're not writing any code or specs yet. You're having a conversation with
ChatGPT or Claude to pressure-test an idea before you commit time to
building it.

## How to run it

1. Open [`prompt.md`](prompt.md) in this folder.
2. Fill in the blanks with your own idea — a rough one-liner is enough to
   start.
3. Paste the filled-in prompt into ChatGPT or Claude.
4. Actually engage with the follow-up questions it asks — this stage only
   works if you answer honestly instead of accepting the first draft.
5. When you're done, you should be able to state, in one or two sentences
   each: who has this problem, what they do today, and what "better" looks
   like for them.

See [`example.md`](example.md) for what this looks like worked through for
**CampusPulse**, the campus events + RSVP app used as the running example in
this workshop.

## What "done" looks like

You don't need a polished problem statement. You need clear, specific
answers to:

- **Who** — a specific person or small group, not "everyone"
- **Today** — what they currently do instead (even if it's messy or manual)
- **Pain** — where that current approach actually breaks down for them
- **Better** — what a small win would look like, in their words if possible

Carry these answers into [Stage 2 — Blueprint](../02-blueprint), where they
become a lightweight spec.
