# Worked example — CampusPulse

**One-line idea:** "An app to help students find campus events."

## The conversation (condensed)

**AI:** Who specifically has this problem — is it every student, or a
particular group?

**Me:** I guess... first-years? People new to campus.

**AI:** What do first-years currently do today when they want to find out
what's happening on campus? Is there an existing channel — a newsletter, a
group chat, posters?

**Me:** There's an events page on the university website, but nobody checks
it. Most people find out about stuff from friends, or a club's Instagram, or
just seeing a poster and forgetting about it by the time it happens.

**AI:** So the current approach is informal and scattered — Instagram,
posters, word of mouth. Where does that actually break down? What's the
moment someone gets frustrated?

**Me:** Probably when they hear about something cool *after* it already
happened. Or they wanted to go to something but forgot the time and missed
it.

**AI:** That's a specific, concrete failure — hearing about it too late, or
forgetting it once they've heard. What would "better" look like for them?
Not features yet — just what changes for them.

**Me:** They'd actually know what's happening that matches stuff they care
about, and they wouldn't lose track of things they said yes to.

## Resulting problem statement

> First-year students at a university don't have a reliable way to discover
> campus events that match their interests. Today, they rely on scattered,
> passive channels — a rarely-checked university events page, club
> Instagram accounts, and word of mouth — which means they often hear about
> events too late, or forget ones they meant to attend. A small win would be
> a single place that surfaces events matched to what they actually care
> about, and reminds them of what they've already said yes to.

Carried into Stage 2: **who** = first-years discovering campus events,
**today** = scattered/passive channels, **pain** = hearing too late /
forgetting, **better** = one matched feed + a reliable record of what
they've RSVP'd to.
