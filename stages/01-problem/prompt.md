# Stage 1 prompt — dig into the problem

Copy this into ChatGPT or Claude and fill in the bracketed parts. Keep your
first pass rough — the point of this prompt is to have the AI push back and
ask you questions, not to hand it a finished idea.

```
I have a rough idea for a small app and I want to stress-test the problem
behind it before I build anything. Here's my one-line idea:

[YOUR ONE-LINE IDEA — e.g. "an app to help students find campus events"]

Before you suggest any features or screens, ask me clarifying questions
about:
1. WHO specifically has this problem — not "everyone," a specific group
2. WHAT they do today instead, even if it's informal or manual
   (a group chat, a spreadsheet, word of mouth, nothing at all)
3. WHERE that current approach breaks down for them — the actual moment
   of friction or frustration
4. WHAT a small, believable win would look like for them

Ask me one question at a time. Push back if my answers are vague or if
I jump ahead to describing features — bring me back to the problem. Once
you think we have clear, specific answers to all four, summarize them
back to me in a short problem statement (3-5 sentences) I can use as the
foundation for the rest of this project.
```

## Tips

- **Answer for a real person, even a specific one you know.** "Students" is
  too broad; "a first-year who just moved to campus and doesn't know any
  club events exist" is usable.
- **It's fine if the problem is small.** A small, real problem beats a big,
  vague one — you have 2.5 hours, not 2.5 months.
- **Resist the urge to describe your app.** If you catch yourself saying
  "so the app would have a screen that...", stop and let the AI redirect you
  back to the problem. That's Stage 2's job, not this one.

When you're happy with the problem statement the AI gives you, save it —
you'll hand it straight into [Stage 2](../02-blueprint/prompt.md).
